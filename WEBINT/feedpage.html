Our Services

RunFunding is an enormous global community built around sport and charity projects. Lots of people from every continent on earth have joined a RunFunding project.

RunFunding helps charity organizers, sport communities find the resources and support them the flatform to organize and to track easily their event. To date, lots of running projects — big and small — have successed with the support of the Run Funding community.

Organization & Maintaince

Create upcoming events using a friendly format with various custom. Fast, easy setup in minutes not a day

Promotion

Easily promote your event on Facebook and other sites. Invite people to your event, and reach a new audience with our promotional tools.

Funding

many famous organizer use RunFunding to creatively unlock the power of their crowd and raise hundreds of millions of dollars to support positive social missions.​

Fitness

One of the great things about running is that almost everyone can do it - start out with nothing more than a pair of trainers. Running provides people with the opportunity to improve their health and fitness.

Communities

You can join RunFunding communities to find people who are interested in the same things you are, running, swimming or riding. Moreover, you and others will do great thing to support posstively to big social missions.

Activity Tracking

lets you count the calories that you consume everyday as well as tally up the calories you expend, and then see if they balance. Now get motivated to get active!

85-year-old Ed Whitlock sets world record at Toronto marathon

An THAN 6:49 AM Lifstyle

Milton, Ont., octogenarian and champion runner Ed Whitlock felt "great relief" upon crossing the finish line of Toronto's Scotiabank Waterfront Marathon in record time on Sunday. "I had real apprehension about how I was going to finish at around halfway," Whitlock told Metro Morning host Matt Galloway. Despite his fears, the 85-year-old Whitlock still managed to set a world record for his age group Sunday. He finished the marathon in just three hours, 56 minutes, and 33.2 seconds.
